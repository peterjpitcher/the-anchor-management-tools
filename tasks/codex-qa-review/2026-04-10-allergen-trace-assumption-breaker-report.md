6. **The upgrade GP formula is correct for multiple independent upgrades, but wrong/ambiguous for grouped upgrade prices.** Severity: Medium. Spec claim: add max cost per `upgrade` group, then “add all `upgrade_price` values to the selling price” in [2026-04-10-allergen-traceability-design.md](/Users/peterpitcher/Cursor/OJ-AnchorManagementTools/docs/superpowers/specs/2026-04-10-allergen-traceability-design.md#L83). Reality: for two separate upgrades like sweet potato fries `+2` and bacon `+2`, your formula is right: `(selling_price + 2 + 2 - total_cost) / (selling_price + 2 + 2)`. But for grouped alternatives, “add all `upgrade_price` values” overstates revenue, because one customer cannot buy every option in the same upgrade group at once. Recommended fix: define revenue aggregation with the same grouping rule as cost: one selected price per upgrade group, plus all ungrouped additive upgrades.

7. **Free swaps can reduce GP, and that is mathematically correct, but the spec should say so explicitly.** Severity: Low. Spec claim: zero-price upgrades are allowed, including steak cut chips at `£0`, in [2026-04-10-allergen-traceability-design.md](/Users/peterpitcher/Cursor/OJ-AnchorManagementTools/docs/superpowers/specs/2026-04-10-allergen-traceability-design.md#L45) and [2026-04-10-allergen-traceability-design.md](/Users/peterpitcher/Cursor/OJ-AnchorManagementTools/docs/superpowers/specs/2026-04-10-allergen-traceability-design.md#L223). Reality: if the free swap costs more, GP should go down; that is not a formula bug, it is the economic truth of offering a free premium swap. Recommended fix: keep the formula, but add explicit acceptance criteria and tests that zero-price upgrades may lower GP and may need separate UI treatment from paid upsells.

The biggest unresolved design choice is recipe atomicity. If the spec does not decide whether a linked recipe is a single removable/swappable component or a nested tree of traceable ingredients, the allergen and `is_modifiable_for` logic will stay ambiguous.
