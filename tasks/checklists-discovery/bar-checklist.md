# The Anchor, Bar Checklist (as supplied 2026-07-17)

Source: owner's brief, verbatim content preserved. This is the working master list for the
new Checklists function. It is expected to be refined and simplified, treat it as a
snapshot, not the final list.

Wording has not been corrected or reworded. Typos in the original are flagged in
[Notes for refinement](#notes-for-refinement) rather than silently fixed.

---

| 2 | Left Bottle Fridge | Temperature |
| 3 | Right Bottle Fridge | Temperature |

---

## 2. Daily, Opening

Done every day when we open.

1. All machines switched on and cleaned (all TVs, fruit machine, and jukebox)
2. Hoover Carpet Area
3. Spot Mop throughout floors (blue bucket)
4. Make sure all spaces (inside and out) are clean and tidy (cigarette butts, glasses, etc)
5. Replace Table Numbers and Beer Mats
6. Menus ready (WhatsApp Pete if you need more)
7. Load ice bucket
8. Utensils out (tongs / measures / cocktail equip)
9. Bin liners in all bins (including 2 in the garden)
10. Glasswasher is put together and turned on
11. Draught & post mix nozzles & drip trays set up
12. Bar top clean (not sticky)
13. Bar pumps are clean (not dusty), and font lights are turned on
14. The back bar stocked and clean with no clutter i.e. Red Wine
15. Fill the jug with water
16. Restock Caddies (Mayo, Cultery Clean, Condaments Clean, Napkins)
17. Open bathroom windows
18. Check Till for Bookings and Place Chalkboards

**Seasonal, Autumn/Winter only:**

19. Load candles into holders and light at open

---

## 3. During service, every two hours

Cleaning checks run at fixed times while we are open.

| Time | Applies |
|------|---------|
| 18:00 Cleaning Check | Every day |
| 20:00 Cleaning Check | Every day |
| 22:00 Cleaning Check | **Fri–Sat only** |

**Instruction shown to staff on each of these checks:**

> Regular Cleaning Check Points: Toilet (including toilet rolls and paper towels in
> holders and handwash topped up), Jukebox, Pool Table, Fruit Machine, Till and door
> push plates.

---

## 4. Daily, Closing

Done every day when we close.

1. All tables clean and beer mats removes (dispose of rubbish ones)
2. All food items behind the bar (including juice) date dotted in the fridge
3. Bottle up including back bar
4. All glassware is washed & on the correct shelves
5. Glasswasher, striped and cleaned as well as collection tray and basket
6. The front & back bar cleaned
7. Snacks restocked to bar
8. Tongs, board, fruit knife, measurers and shakers all washed up and left to dry
9. The fruit tray washed up and left to dry
10. All bins emptied & bins left without a bag (let the bin air out overnight)
11. All rubbish removed from the building (including from celler)
12. Cloths, tea towels and bar runners in the washing basket
13. Draft & post mix nozzles removed and left in soda
14. Drip trays cleaned & replaced
15. Sinks cleaned & free of rubbish
16. All machines & music switched off (**10 pm Sun–Thu / 12 am Fri–Sat**)
17. Wash beer mats through glass washer and leave to dry over radiator
18. Sweep and mop behind the bar (blue bucket)
19. Sweep floors throughout
20. Mop, clean and 'Harpic' both bathrooms (red bucket)
21. Close and lock all windows and doors

## 5. Between daily and weekly, variable cadence

These need doing more often than weekly but not every day. Each one varies; none has a
fixed day. The exact interval per item is **still to be decided**.

1. Wipe chairs and table and legs
2. Wipe pool table and legs down
3. Clean glass racks
4. Clean display bottles and shelves
5. Window seals wiped and windows cleaned
6. Brush pool table (**only spray once a week**)
7. Stock rotation
8. Refill caddies
9. Freshen Pub Cleanliness
10. Glass Clean Jukebox
11. Restock fridges, snacks, bottles, and rotate old stock
12. Hoover/mop

---

## Cadence types this list proves we need

The bar checklist alone requires the system to handle:

| Cadence | Example from this list |
|---------|------------------------|
| Daily, value required | Cellar cooler temperature |
| Daily at open | Hoover carpet area |
| Daily at close | Close and lock all windows and doors |
| Fixed times during service | 18:00 / 20:00 cleaning checks |
| Fixed times, certain days only | 22:00 cleaning check (Fri–Sat) |
| Seasonal | Candles (Autumn/Winter) |
| Variable, roughly every few days | Wipe chairs and table legs |
| Weekly, tied to a more frequent task | Spray pool table (once a week) vs brush (more often) |

The owner's brief also names **annual, quarterly, monthly, bi-weekly and weekly** cadences
for other checklists not yet listed. The model must cover those too.

## Notes for refinement

Flagged for the owner, **not changed**:

1. **Typos in the source list**, "Cultery" (Cutlery), "Condaments" (Condiments),
   "beer mats removes" (removed), "Glasswasher, striped" (stripped), "celler" (cellar),
   "Draft" vs "Draught" (used both ways). Worth correcting before these become live
   database records staff read every day.
2. **"Refill caddies" appears twice**, opening list item 16 ("Restock Caddies") and
   variable list item 8 ("Refill caddies"). Are these the same job, or is one a top-up and
   the other a deep restock?
3. **"Hoover/mop" appears twice**, opening items 2–3 (hoover carpet, spot mop) and
   variable list item 12. Same question.
4. **"Freshen Pub Cleanliness"** is not a specific instruction. A staff member cannot know
   whether they have done it. Needs defining or dropping.
5. **"Brush pool table (only spray once a week)"** is two tasks in one row: a frequent
   brush and a weekly spray. Probably should be split.
6. **Closing item 16** hard-codes machine-off times (10pm Sun–Thu / 12am Fri–Sat). If
   opening hours change, this text goes stale. Consider driving it from opening hours.
7. **Opening item 6** ("WhatsApp Pete if you need more") embeds a person and a channel in
   the task text. Fine for now; flagged because it will need changing when Pete's not the
   contact.
8. **List length**, opening is 19 items, closing is 21. Checklist research consistently
   finds long lists get pencil-whipped. Simplification is already on the owner's mind and
   is the right instinct.
